Interesting Facts

London's Name

What you think today of the amazing city has a long and not known history. Some standing theories state that London was built by Romans around 50 AD. The origin of its name also has a puzzling history. Throughout history, London used to have different names, including Londonium, Ludenwic, and Ludenberg. The most accepted version is that the name for London has derived from the old Celtic word “Londinous” which means to be bold.

Red Buses

Red buses are for sure the most typical thing that your mind associates with London. The iconic vehicles didn’t always have this color. Once upon a time, each of them had a different color signifying a different route. To stand out from the competition, the most prominent company of buses, London General Omnibus, decided to paint its buses red in 1907. Ever since then, London buses have been red.

Cab Drivers

It takes a strong knowledge of the area, patience, and a license, to become a cab driver. However, in London, the bar to qualify is far higher. London cab drivers face a notoriously difficult application and approval processes. They must take the London Knowledge test, including knowing 25,000 streets and all the landmarks within 6 miles of Charing Cross.
